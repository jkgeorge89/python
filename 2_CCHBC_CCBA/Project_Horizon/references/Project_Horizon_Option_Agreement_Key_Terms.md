# Project Horizon Option Agreement - Key Terms Summary

Source reviewed in full: `agreements/28. Project Horizon - Option Agreement (Agreed form).docx`

This note summarises the operative terms of the Option Agreement relevant to the Call Option and Put Option. It is a working commercial summary, not a substitute for legal advice or for the full contract text.

## 1. Parties and Subject Matter

- Titan: European Refreshments Unlimited Company.
- Titan Guarantor: The Coca-Cola Company.
- Olympus BV: Coca-Cola HBC Holdings B.V.
- Company: Coca-Cola Beverages Africa (Pty) Ltd.
- Base Option Shares at signing: 1,386,020 E ordinary shares, equal to 25% of the issued share capital.
- Defined `Option Shares` are broader than the base block. They also include derived securities, replacement securities after capital reorganisations, and any shares Titan owns in the Company from time to time.

## 2. High-Level Structure

- Titan grants Olympus BV an irrevocable Call Option.
- Olympus BV grants Titan an irrevocable Put Option.
- Both options are governed by notice, approval, completion, suspension, and lapse mechanics.
- During the overlap period, the earlier notice prevails under the `One Live Notice` rule.
- The agreement terminates on the earlier of:
  - Titan ceasing to hold any shares in the Company because of an option exercise.
  - The sixth anniversary of SPA Completion Date, subject to any pending option completion.
  - Written agreement of all parties.

## 3. Call Option Summary

### 3.1 Grant and Timing

- Clause 2 grants Olympus BV an irrevocable right to purchase the Option Shares for the Purchase Price during the `Call Option Period`.
- `Call Option Period` is defined as running from SPA Completion Date to the fifth anniversary, excluding any `Call Option Suspension Period`.
- Clause 4.1 adds an important exercise gate: the Call Option may only be exercised after the expiry of the third anniversary of SPA Completion Date.

Practical effect:

- Legally, the call exists from SPA completion.
- Economically, it is not exercisable until after year 3.
- Any suspension does not extend the call beyond the fifth anniversary.

### 3.2 Scope of Exercise

- The call is not always a simple single-shot right.
- If full `Option Antitrust Approval` for acquisition of the entire share capital was not granted before signing, then:
  - the call can initially be exercised only for all of the Option Shares, not part; but
  - if Olympus BV later obtains the relevant antitrust approvals for all Option Shares on or before the first exercise, then it may exercise for all or part, in multiple exercises over time.
- If full `Option Antitrust Approval` for the entire share capital was already granted before signing, the call may be exercised for all or part from the outset, including by multiple exercises over time.

### 3.3 Call Exercise Constraints

For each partial Call Option Exercise:

- It cannot be for fewer shares than 5% of the aggregate issued share capital of the Company.
- It cannot leave Titan holding less than 7% of the aggregate issued share capital unless the exercise takes Titan to zero.
- Olympus BV may acquire through a nominee within the Olympus Group, subject to customary KYC.

### 3.4 Call Conditions and Lapse

- Completion after a call notice is conditional on mandatory shareholder and regulatory approvals, including the SA Takeover Regulation Panel waiver if required.
- The parties must use reasonable endeavours and cooperate to obtain required approvals.
- `Option Completion Date` is normally the twentieth business day after the Call Option Exercise Notice, or the twentieth business day after required approvals are obtained.
- Hard stop: completion may not occur later than 12 months from the Call Option Exercise Notice unless Olympus BV and Titan agree otherwise in writing.
- If the conditions are not satisfied by the Option Completion Date, the relevant Call Option Exercise lapses.

### 3.5 Call Notice Mechanics

- Exercise is made by a `Call Option Exercise Notice`.
- The notice must specify:
  - the Exercise Shares;
  - the Per Share Purchase Price;
  - the total Purchase Price.
- Once served, the notice is irrevocable unless Titan consents in writing.

## 4. Put Option Summary

### 4.1 Grant and Timing

- Clause 5 grants Titan an irrevocable right to sell the Option Shares to Olympus BV for the Purchase Price during the `Put Option Period`.
- `Put Option Period` runs from expiry of three and a half years after SPA Completion Date to the sixth anniversary, excluding any `Put Option Suspension Period`.
- Any suspension does not extend the put beyond the sixth anniversary.

### 4.2 Scope of Exercise

- Titan may exercise the put at any time during the Put Option Period.
- The put must be exercised for all, and not part, of the Option Shares held by Titan at that time.
- This is materially tighter than the call: Titan has no contractual ability to do staged partial put exercises.
- Olympus BV may nominate a group nominee to acquire the shares, subject to customary KYC.

### 4.3 Put Conditions and Lapse

- Completion after a put notice is conditional on mandatory shareholder and regulatory approvals, including the SA Takeover Regulation Panel waiver if required.
- The parties must use reasonable endeavours and cooperate to obtain approvals.
- `Option Completion Date` is normally the twentieth business day after the Put Option Exercise Notice, or the twentieth business day after required approvals are obtained.
- Hard stop: completion may not occur later than 12 months from the Put Option Exercise Notice unless Olympus BV and Titan agree otherwise in writing.
- If the conditions are not satisfied by the Option Completion Date, the relevant Put Option Exercise lapses.

### 4.4 Put Notice Mechanics

- Exercise is made by a `Put Option Exercise Notice`.
- The notice must specify:
  - the Exercise Shares, which cannot be fewer than all shares Titan then holds;
  - the Per Share Purchase Price;
  - the total Purchase Price.
- Once served, the notice is irrevocable unless Olympus BV consents in writing.

## 5. Overlap Period and One Live Notice

- `Overlap Period` runs from expiry of three and a half years after SPA Completion Date through the fifth anniversary.
- During this period both call and put rights can be live.
- Clause 8 provides that the earlier `Option Exercise Notice` is the `Prior Notice` and prevails for the shares specified in that notice.
- Any later notice is automatically reduced by the shares already captured by the Prior Notice, even if that reduction is not stated expressly and even if completion of the Prior Notice has not yet occurred.

Practical effect:

- The agreement is not just two independent options.
- It contains explicit interdependence during the overlap window.
- The first valid notice can materially constrain the second party's remaining exercise capacity.

## 6. Purchase Price Mechanics

### 6.1 Core Formula

Per Share Purchase Price equals:

- SPA price per ordinary share as at SPA Completion Date;
- less deductions for certain claim-related adjustments and Per Share Gaia Leakage Amount;
- plus the `Applicable Coupon`.

The Per Share Purchase Price is rounded down to two decimal places.

### 6.2 Applicable Coupon

The agreement defines the coupon as USD interest, capitalised annually, from SPA Completion Date until but excluding the relevant Option Completion Date.

Call coupon schedule:

- Year 0-1: 2.75%
- Year 1-2: 3.00%
- Year 2-3: 3.00%
- Year 3-4: 4.20%
- Year 4-5: 4.20%

Put coupon schedule:

- Year 0-1: 2.75%
- Year 1-2: 3.00%
- Year 2-3: 3.00%
- Year 3-4: 4.10%
- Year 4-5: 4.10%
- Year 5-6: 4.10%

### 6.3 True-Up to Actual Completion Date

- Five business days before completion, Olympus BV must issue a further notice confirming the Per Share Purchase Price using the Applicable Coupon through the actual Option Completion Date.
- That means the final strike is not fixed on notice date alone; it is updated to reflect the actual completion date.

### 6.4 Other Economic Adjustments

- Dividends paid by the Company to Titan between SPA Completion Date and Option Completion Date reduce the Purchase Price.
- Outstanding finally determined SPA claims may be set off against the Purchase Price at Olympus BV's discretion.
- Certain adjustable SPA claims can also reduce the price through the `Per Share Claim Adjustment` mechanism.
- Olympus BV may elect to satisfy all or part of the Purchase Price in Olympus AG shares instead of cash, subject to the contractual conditions in Clause 11.4.

## 7. Completion and Transfer Mechanics

- Completion is virtual unless otherwise agreed.
- Olympus BV is not obliged to buy any Exercise Shares unless all Exercise Shares in that exercise complete simultaneously.
- Titan transfers legal and beneficial ownership with full title guarantee, free from encumbrances, together with attached rights.
- Risk, benefit, and ownership pass on the Option Completion Date.

## 8. Suspension Mechanics

- Both call and put can be suspended if a legally binding governmental order, writ, ruling, notice, or similar decision makes the relevant option illegal or prohibits consummation of the sale.
- Suspension ends when the relevant impediment is no longer effective.
- The suspension definition is symmetrical for call and put, but the outer long-stop dates are different:
  - call cannot extend beyond year 5;
  - put cannot extend beyond year 6.

## 9. Valuation-Relevant Observations

These points matter for modelling and should not be lost in shorthand summaries:

- The call is not a plain vanilla American-style right from time 0. It only becomes exercisable after year 3, despite the defined Call Option Period starting at SPA completion.
- The put is not exercisable until year 3.5 and is all-or-nothing for Titan's then-held shares.
- The call may be partially exercisable and repeatable, but only subject to antitrust approval status and tranche constraints.
- The put is more rigid than the call because Titan cannot stage partial exits under the contractual put mechanic.
- The overlap period creates game-like interaction because the first live notice constrains later notices.
- The final economic strike depends on the actual completion date, not just the exercise notice date, because coupon accrual runs to completion.
- Regulatory approval risk is embedded directly in the contract through the 12-month long-stop and lapse mechanics.

## 10. Bottom-Line Takeaways

- From Olympus BV's perspective, the agreement delivers a delayed-start call with potential for partial and multiple exercises, subject to approval status and tranche rules.
- From Titan's perspective, the agreement delivers a delayed-start put, but only on an all-or-nothing basis for shares still held at the time.
- The two options are contractually linked during the overlap period through the One Live Notice rule.
- Any valuation that reduces the agreement to two fully independent standard options will miss important legal mechanics.
